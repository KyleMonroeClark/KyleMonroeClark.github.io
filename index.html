About Me

Hello! My name is Kyle Clark, and I’m a software and data enthusiast currently based in San Luis Obispo, CA. I’m passionate about solving complex problems and using data to drive meaningful change. Whether it's building systems to streamline processes or analyzing data to inform key decisions, I thrive on turning challenges into actionable solutions. I’m open to new opportunities and willing to relocate for the right role.

Like many people, I began my professional journey in the restaurant industry while attending college. I started as a busser and quickly rose to the role of shift supervisor. This fast progression became a theme throughout my career—at every job I’ve had, my ability to make significant improvements and drive positive change was quickly recognized. This mindset of efficiency, problem-solving, and continuous improvement carried over as I moved into more technically-focused roles.

My first significant step in the tech space came as a test proctor for an IT company, Clever Ducks, where I worked closely with a team of technically skilled individuals. This role taught me how to bridge the gap between technical and non-technical audiences, ensuring clear communication between the two groups and making complex information more accessible.

At Hilton Grand Vacations, I gained deeper exposure to business decision-making on a large scale. I was involved in corporate reporting, departmental budgeting, and played a key role in building dashboards that communicated data-driven insights to non-technical stakeholders, including the timeshare owner's board of directors. It was during this time that I truly saw the power of data in driving business strategy and outcomes.

Currently, I’m applying those skills as a data analyst at a healthcare clinic, where I work tirelessly to gather, organize, clean, and transform data into easily digestible reports and key performance indicators (KPIs). The goal is always to drive effective business decisions—whether that’s improving patient care or enhancing operational efficiencies.

At every stage of my career, I’ve embraced challenges with a mindset that no problem is too big to solve. I believe it’s about breaking problems into manageable pieces, making informed decisions, and staying determined to find solutions, even when the answers aren't immediately visible.

Looking forward, my goal is to continue growing as both a decision-maker and a decision-informer. I want to tackle complex problems that require creative, data-driven solutions and contribute to an organization’s success in a meaningful way.

Please feel free to check out my projects, my GitHub, and the technical skills and areas of expertise I’ve listed. Thank you for your time, and I look forward to connecting!
